### 👤 My Vision

I plan to spend at least 2–3 years in an entry-level IT position, focusing on understanding the company's standards, culture, objectives, and workflows. During this time, my priority will be to build a strong professional foundation while adapting to the responsibilities and expectations of the role.

I also want to work alongside experienced professionals, contribute positively to my team, and learn from people with different backgrounds and expertise. I believe that strong communication and teamwork are just as important as technical knowledge in building a successful career.

At the same time, I intend to continuously improve my technical skills and deepen my knowledge in the areas of IT that I consider essential. Technology evolves constantly, and I believe continuous learning is one of the most important responsibilities of an IT professional. While I remain open to new opportunities, I expect to gradually specialize in the fields that best match my interests and strengths.

Alongside my professional growth, I will also dedicate time to developing my personal projects. Whenever possible, I also hope to contribute to open-source projects and create tools that other developers can learn from or benefit from.

I see this period as the inevitable "workaholic phase" of building a successful career prioritizing my work, learning, and future over leisure whenever necessary.



After gaining around three years of experience, my goal is to earn a promotion or move into a more ambitious and rewarding role, where I hope to continue growing professionally for at least another eight years. As I gain more experience, I also hope to mentor junior, share the knowledge I have acquired, and help create a positive and collaborative working environment.

I want my work to solve real problems, provide value to the people who use it, and contribute to the success of the company I work for. I value responsibility, discipline, consistency, and professionalism in everything I do.

At that stage, I also want to achieve a healthier work-life balance, allowing me to enjoy my free time outside of work more frequently while maintaining high standards in my professional career.



Around 2029, I plan to publish some of my personal projects and launch others to the public. My long-term objective is to turn these projects into a part-time source of income. Even if I already have a stable and successful career, I believe it's important to think ahead. While many people only think about retirement once they reach it, I want to prepare in advance by growing my personal projects while I'm still working, so that they can eventually become meaningful hobbies or even a small business during my retirement.
